# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads ensure product quality through comprehensive testing strategies, automation, and quality standards. They collaborate with developers and product teams to validate that features meet acceptance criteria and maintain high reliability.

### Responsibilities
- Design and execute test plans for features and releases
- Build and maintain automated test suites
- Perform manual testing where automation is not practical
- Document and triage defects with clear reproduction steps
- Validate acceptance criteria before marking work as complete
- Review quality metrics and recommend improvements

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality, reliable features
- Reduce bugs reaching production
- Maintain fast feedback loops during development

### Typical Communication
- Acceptance criteria review in planning
- Daily standups on blockers and test progress
- Defect reports and quality metrics

---

## Engineering Manager / Tech Lead

### Role Summary
Engineering Managers and Tech Leads provide technical oversight, mentorship, and architectural guidance. They ensure solutions are technically sound, scalable, and align with system architecture.

### Responsibilities
- Review technical designs and architecture decisions
- Mentor developers and guide code quality standards
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigations
- Participate in planning to assess technical feasibility
- Advocate for technical debt reduction and refactoring
- Ensure adherence to coding standards and best practices

### Goals
- Maintain system health and scalability
- Build a strong, capable engineering team
- Make technically sound decisions that balance quality and speed

### Typical Communication
- Design review meetings
- Code review feedback
- Technical architecture discussions
- Career development conversations with team members

---

## Security Lead

### Role Summary
Security Leads ensure that projects follow security best practices, compliance requirements, and incident response protocols. They partner with developers and operations to build security into every stage of delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Review feature designs for security implications
- Configure and monitor security scanning in CI/CD
- Respond to and triage security incidents
- Define security acceptance criteria
- Conduct security reviews before releases
- Maintain incident response playbooks
- Communicate security vulnerabilities and mitigations

### Goals
- Prevent security breaches and data loss
- Enable secure-by-default delivery practices
- Respond quickly and effectively to security incidents

### Typical Communication
- Security requirements in acceptance criteria
- Security incident alerts and escalations
- CI/CD security scan results
- Post-incident retrospectives

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate the deployment process, maintain release documentation, and ensure smooth transitions to production. They work with engineering, QA, and operations to manage release timelines and rollback scenarios.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain release plans and schedules
- Prepare release notes and deployment documentation
- Coordinate pre-release testing and validation
- Execute or oversee deployment to production
- Verify post-deployment health and metrics
- Trigger rollback procedures if needed
- Communicate release status to stakeholders

### Goals
- Deliver releases with minimal risk and downtime
- Maintain clear communication throughout the release process
- Enable quick and safe rollback if issues arise

### Typical Communication
- Release notes and deployment guides
- Pre-deployment checklists and sign-offs
- Stakeholder release announcements
- Post-release verification reports

---

## Scrum Master / Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach teams on agile practices. They help teams maintain a sustainable pace and continuously improve their process.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, planning, and retrospectives
- Help remove blockers and impediments
- Ensure the team follows agreed agile practices
- Coach the team on continuous improvement
- Maintain the project board and sprint metrics
- Support cross-team communication
- Escalate systemic issues that block delivery

### Goals
- Enable high-performing, self-organizing teams
- Maintain sustainable delivery pace
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement

### Typical Communication
- Sprint ceremonies (standups, planning, retros)
- One-on-ones with team members
- Process improvement suggestions
- Velocity and burndown metrics

---

## Stakeholder / Sponsor

### Role Summary
Stakeholders and Sponsors represent business interests, provide approvals, and ensure projects align with organizational strategy. They are decision-makers who prioritize and fund initiatives.

### Responsibilities
- Approve project charter and business case
- Provide strategic direction and priorities
- Make trade-off decisions on scope, time, and budget
- Review milestone and release progress
- Remove organizational blockers
- Communicate project status to executive leadership

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver business value
- Maintain alignment with organizational strategy
- Enable timely decision-making and approvals

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder updates
- Milestone reviews and sign-offs
- Ad-hoc escalations and decisions
- Executive briefings on outcomes
